## The Problem: ₦4.2 Trillion Lost Annually

Nigeria's informal economy represents **65% of GDP** and employs over **80 million people**, yet these workers face systematic financial exclusion:

| Pain Point | Scale | Annual Economic Impact |
|------------|-------|------------------------|
| **Gig workers paid late or never** | 20M+ workers | ₦800B in delayed/lost wages |
| **Ajo/Esusu fraud and defaults** | ₦2.4T in informal savings | ₦480B lost to fraud annually |
| **Credit invisibility** | 60M+ unbanked adults | ₦2.9T in foregone economic activity |
| **Predatory lending** | 15M+ borrowers | ₦200B in excess interest paid |

**Total addressable pain: ₦4.2 trillion annually**

The root cause: Nigeria's financial infrastructure was built for formal employment and excludes those who hustle.

| Moat Type | HustleX Advantage | Defensibility |
|-----------|-------------------|---------------|
| **Data Moat** | Proprietary credit data from gigs + savings | High - takes years to replicate |
