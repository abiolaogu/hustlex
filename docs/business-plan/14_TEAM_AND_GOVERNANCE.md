## Compensation Philosophy

### Principles

1. **Below-market cash, above-market equity** - We're a startup; equity is the upside
2. **Transparency** - Clear bands, fair negotiation
3. **Performance-linked** - Bonuses tied to company and individual OKRs
4. **Equity for all** - Every full-time employee gets equity

## Culture and Values

### Culture Principles

1. **User Obsession**
   - Every decision starts with "How does this help our users?"
   - Regular user research and feedback loops
   - Celebrate user success stories

2. **Ownership Mentality**
   - Act like an owner, because you are (equity!)
   - Take initiative, don't wait for permission
   - Accountable for outcomes, not just activities

3. **Speed with Quality**
   - Move fast, but don't break things (especially in fintech)
   - Ship frequently, learn quickly
   - Quality is non-negotiable for financial services

4. **Radical Transparency**
   - Share information openly by default
   - Honest feedback, delivered kindly
   - Metrics and decisions visible to all

5. **Inclusive Excellence**
   - Diverse perspectives make better products
   - High standards for everyone
   - Meritocracy in opportunity and recognition

### Cultural Rituals

| Ritual | Frequency | Purpose |
|--------|-----------|---------|
| All-hands meeting | Weekly (30 min) | Alignment, celebration |
| Demo day | Bi-weekly | Showcase work, get feedback |
| User story sharing | Weekly | Stay connected to users |
| Team retrospective | Monthly | Continuous improvement |
| Founder office hours | Weekly | Open door, ask anything |
| Team social | Monthly | Build relationships |

## Decision-Making Framework

### Decision Types

| Type | Definition | Who Decides | Process |
|------|------------|-------------|---------|
| **Type 1** | Irreversible, high-stakes | CEO/Board | Careful analysis, multiple inputs |
| **Type 2** | Reversible, lower-stakes | Relevant owner | Decide quickly, iterate |
| **Type 3** | Day-to-day operations | Individual | Just do it |

### RAPID Framework

For significant decisions:

| Role | Responsibility |
|------|----------------|
| **R**ecommend | Proposes decision and analysis |
| **A**gree | Must agree for decision to proceed |
| **P**erform | Implements the decision |
| **I**nput | Consulted for expertise |
| **D**ecide | Single person who makes the call |

### Meeting Cadence

| Meeting | Frequency | Attendees | Duration | Purpose |
|---------|-----------|-----------|----------|---------|
| Daily standup | Daily | All | 15 min | Sync, blockers |
| Leadership sync | Weekly | C-suite | 60 min | Strategy, decisions |
| All-hands | Weekly | All | 30 min | Updates, alignment |
| 1:1s | Weekly | Manager/report | 30 min | Coaching, feedback |
| Board meeting | Quarterly | Board | 2-3 hours | Governance |
| OKR review | Quarterly | All | Half-day | Planning |

## Performance Management

### OKR Framework

**Company OKRs:**
- Set quarterly by leadership
- 3-5 objectives, 3-5 key results each
- Shared at all-hands

**Team OKRs:**
- Derived from company OKRs
- Set by team leads
- Reviewed weekly

**Individual OKRs:**
- Aligned to team OKRs
- Set collaboratively with manager
- 60% OKRs, 40% role responsibilities

### Review Cycle

| Event | Frequency | Focus |
|-------|-----------|-------|
| Weekly 1:1 | Weekly | Progress, blockers, coaching |
| OKR check-in | Bi-weekly | KR progress, adjustments |
| Quarterly review | Quarterly | OKR scoring, feedback |
| Annual review | Annual | Compensation, promotion |
| 360 feedback | Annual | Comprehensive feedback |

### Performance Rating

| Rating | Definition | Distribution Target |
|--------|------------|---------------------|
| Exceptional | Consistently exceeds, role model | ~10% |
| Strong | Meets all, exceeds some | ~30% |
| Solid | Meets expectations | ~50% |
| Developing | Below expectations, improving | ~10% |
| Underperforming | Significantly below, PIP | <5% |
